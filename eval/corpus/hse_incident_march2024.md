## Raw Concept

A near-miss incident occurred on March 12, 2024 during pressure test preparation operations on Well A-14. A technician opened the wrong valve sequence while rigging up the test manifold, resulting in an uncontrolled release of residual pressure from the kill line. The release was contained within the well cellar area and no personnel were exposed to the discharge. Root cause analysis identified a procedural deviation — the technician did not follow the valve alignment checklist and instead relied on memory from a previous operation on a different well. Corrective actions include mandatory two-person valve alignment verification, color-coded valve identification tags on all test manifold connections, and refresher training for all well services personnel. The incident was classified as a high-potential near-miss.
