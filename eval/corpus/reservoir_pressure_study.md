## Raw Concept

The reservoir pressure decline curve analysis covers the main producing interval from 9,800 to 10,200 feet TVD. Initial reservoir pressure was 4,850 psi at datum depth, now declined to approximately 3,200 psi after 6 years of production. The depletion model indicates a partial aquifer support mechanism providing approximately 40% pressure maintenance, with the remaining decline driven by fluid withdrawal. Current production is operating above the bubble point pressure of 2,900 psi, but the forecast indicates the field will reach bubble point by Q4 2025 if production rates are maintained. Infill drilling or pressure maintenance via water injection should be evaluated before reaching this threshold to avoid solution gas drive efficiency losses.
